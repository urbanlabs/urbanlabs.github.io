Howdy!

UrbanLabs.io LLC helps you out with your strategery and analysis problems that support sustainable, efficient, and healthy urban environments.

Issues

Here are a few of the types of issues that I love working on:

  • making public transportation systems that everybody wants to ride
  • creating urban environments that make active transportation safe and convenient
  • eliminating externalities related to climate change
  • incentivizing efficient and affordable land use and development patterns
  • user and impact fees that are simple, understandable, and comprehensive
  • making environmental assessment good for the environment
  • data analysis that adds value to conversations (avoiding data landfills)
  • public processes that add meaningful information and result in better policy

Types of Services

  • Technical advisor to guide team throughout a project or issue
  • Review technical work
  • Develop technical backing for specific policy issue
  • Technical strategic planning
  • Serve as a thought partner and technical guide for management

Team

@e-lo is Elizabeth Sall, outgoing Deputy Director for Technology Services for the San Francisco County Transportation Authority and current free-thinker and do-er. She is excited to have the opportunity to share what she has learned from her combined experiences in software development, urban data analysis, public-sector management, and policy development to a variety of clients and needs.

Elizabeth has specific skills related to:

  • Software Dev: Python, Agile Development, GitHub Flow, Pivotal, and functional (as in I can stumble through it okay) although not in-depth knowledge of AWS, Heroku, C++, HTML, javascript/jQuery, node.js, Gephi
  • Data Analysis Software: Python/Pandas, R, JMP, SAS, SPSS/PSPP, open-source, ESRI and OS GIS
  • Data Mucking & Analysis Techniques: Locational data processing, discrete choices models, network analysis, route choice models, language processing, regression, cluster analysis, & social network analysis.
  • Data sources: transit vehicle (APC and AVL), personal location (GPS, cell phone, bluetooth), network (road, transit, bike, pedestrian such as GTFS, OSM, and proprietary), & surveys (on-board transit, household travel surveys, census). Started collecting data via smartphones back in 2009 with Cycle Tracks.
  • Urban behavior and policies: Extensive experience in policy-sensitive analysis of issues related to congested urban systems such as transit crowding, transit reliability, congested traffic flow, pedestrian travel choices, bicycle travel choices, parking choice, active transportation, GHG emissions, transportation equity & travel demand management strategies.
  • Project management: research projects, planning studies, & policy analyses.
  • Strategic planning for analysis teams: Peer reviews, budgets, toolbox building, data standards, software selection, QA/QC procedures & skill-building.
  • Research: developed research agendas, ongoing relationships with a variety of researchers at a multitude of universities, technical advisory committee chair and panel member overseeing research projects, founder of ResearcHQ.org, extensive peer-reviewed publications and publication review experience, & frequent advice-giver.
  • Outreach and Education: Frequent lecturer and presenter at universities, conferences, & professional organizations.

Elizabeth compliments her professional life with a variety of efforts related to international and local conservation, poverty alleviation, and global health (and particularly where these issues intersect) through vehicles such as impact investing, social entrepreneurship, board service on several organizations and connecting some dots where they need connecting. She has a particular passion for empowering local communities to affect (and hopefully control) their own destiny.
